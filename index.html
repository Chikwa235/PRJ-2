CPR

Summary: Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) is an emergency lifesaving procedure performed when the heart stops beating.

Steps: Check responsiveness, call for help, begin chest compressions (100-120 per minute), give rescue breaths if trained.

Bleeding

Summary: Control bleeding by applying pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or bandage.

Steps: Apply pressure, elevate the area if possible, bandage the wound, do not remove objects stuck in the wound.

Burns

Summary: Treat minor burns by cooling the burn under cool running water for at least 10 minutes.

Steps: Cool the burn, cover loosely with a sterile gauze, avoid ice or ointments, seek medical help for major burns.

Choking

Summary: Help a choking person by performing the Heimlich maneuver.

Steps: Ask if they can speak or cough. If not, give 5 back blows followed by 5 abdominal thrusts.

Fractures

Summary: Immobilize the area and prevent movement to avoid further damage.

Steps: Apply a splint, elevate if possible, avoid realigning the bone, get medical help immediately.

Snake Bite

Summary: Snake bites can be life-threatening depending on the type of snake. Immediate action is required.

Steps: Keep the person calm and still. Immobilize the bitten area. Do not suck out venom or apply a tourniquet. Seek medical help immediately.

Heatstroke

Summary: Heatstroke is a severe condition caused by the body overheating.

Steps: Move to cool place, remove clothing, apply cool cloths or bath. Call emergency services.

Seizures

Summary: Seizures cause uncontrolled movements and loss of awareness. Protect the person from harm.

Steps: Clear area, do not restrain, time seizure, place in recovery position, call help if needed.

Electric Shock

Summary: Electrical injuries can range from minor burns to life-threatening shocks.

Steps: Turn off source, don't touch victim, check for breathing, perform CPR if needed.

Poisoning

Summary: Poisoning can occur through ingestion, inhalation, or skin contact.

Steps: Identify the substance, do not induce vomiting, call poison control.

Drowning

Summary: Drowning is caused by inhaling water, leading to respiratory failure.

Steps: Remove from water, check breathing, perform CPR if needed, call for help.

Anaphylaxis

Summary: Severe allergic reactions require fast treatment with epinephrine.

Steps: Use auto-injector (EpiPen), call emergency services, lay person down, elevate legs.

Asthma Attack

Summary: Asthma attacks cause difficulty in breathing and need quick relief using an inhaler.

Steps: Sit up, use inhaler, call emergency if symptoms persist.

Hypothermia

Summary: Hypothermia occurs when body temperature drops below normal.

Steps: Move to warmth, remove wet clothing, warm with blankets and fluids.
